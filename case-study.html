01 / Project Introduction

Project Introduction

shared problems and challenges i was trying to solve

Students are some of the most creative people on any campus, yet turning that creativity into a real business is messy. Whether they paint, bake, design clothes, or make jewellery, most student creators sell through word of mouth, the odd campus stall, or scattered WhatsApp and Instagram posts. There is no single, trusted place to set up shop, get discovered, and take payments safely.

SmarKet replaces that fragmented experience with one dedicated marketplace, so the journey from student hobbyist to micro entrepreneur is faster, more structured, and far less stressful.

Core Capabilities

  • Dedicated seller storefronts
  • UPI QR + Cash on Delivery
  • Real-time order tracking
  • Ratings, reviews & chat
  • Role-based dashboards

The Problems

For Sellers

  • Scattered, informal selling with no central hub
  • Limited reach beyond close friends
  • No order or inventory management
  • Unsafe, untracked cash / UPI transactions

For Buyers

  • Hard to discover unique peer-made products
  • No trust layer: no ratings, reviews, verification
  • No way to follow an order once placed
  • No room for sellers to grow professionally

"There is no dedicated bridge connecting student creators with the local campus market."

03 / User Research

User Research

showed user research insights and target audience segments

Research combined a review of two peer studies on campus marketplaces with a close look at how students already buy and sell today. The Campus Stalls study, which scored 4.62 / 5 on usability, confirmed that a student-specific marketplace is both wanted and workable.

Key Insights

General marketplaces feel too broad
Trust is the real currency
Visibility, not talent, is the bottleneck
Simplicity beats feature overload
UPI preferred, but needs tracking
High demand confirmed (4.62/5)

Target Audience

Student Creators

Artists, bakers and designers who make handmade goods and want a simple way to sell on campus.

Side Hustle Sellers

Students running a small business to cover living costs, who need reach beyond word of mouth.

Curious Buyers

Students after unique, affordable handmade items from people they already trust on campus.

Campus Community

The entrepreneurship cell, faculty and IT who support and oversee student commerce.

"The sharpest insight: for student creators the bottleneck isn't talent, it's visibility and trust. So SmarKet is built around discovery and credibility, not just transactions."

05 / Requirements

Requirements

defined requirements + prioritized them

User Stories

Aarya · the seller
  • Set up my own storefront and list items with photos, so I look professional and reach beyond my friends.
  • See one dashboard for pending, in-progress, and completed orders, so nothing slips through.
  • Offer secure UPI, card, or COD options, so every transaction feels safe.
Rohan · the buyer
  • Search and filter by category, price, and rating, so I find unique items quickly.
  • Get real-time tracking, so I know when to expect pickup or delivery.
  • See ratings, reviews, and chat with the seller, so I can buy with confidence.

06 / Ideation

Ideation

created basic IA + showed lo-fi wireframes

After signing in, the architecture splits by role: buyers land on the Home Screen while sellers go straight to the Seller Dashboard. Each hub keeps its own navigation shallow and predictable.

Site Map / Information Architecture

User Flows

Buyer Flow

Sign In → Home → Category → Product → Chat → Cart → Payment → Order Tracking

Seller Flow

Sign In → Seller Dashboard → Upload Product → My Products

Interactions & Unhappy Paths

Different ways people interact

  • Browse by category, or search and filter
  • Buy now, or add to bag for later
  • Chat to request a custom, made-to-order piece
  • Pay by UPI / QR or Cash on Delivery
  • Switch roles: shop as a buyer, sell from the dashboard

When things don't go to plan

  • Payment fails → retry or fall back to Cash on Delivery
  • Product out of stock → Add to Bag is disabled
  • No search results → empty state with suggested categories
  • Seller slow to reply → chat shows status, order still possible
  • Order issue → "Need help? Contact seller" on tracking

07 / Solution

Solution & Design Decisions

broke down my final hi-fi solution + design decisions

Warm Marketplace Identity

Challenge: avoid a cold, corporate feel.

Decision: warm cream & gold tones that echo handmade craft.

Impact: approachable, student-friendly experience.

Role-Based Experience

Challenge: buyers, sellers, admins need different things.

Decision: one login, three focused views.

Impact: cleaner, less cluttered interfaces.

Checkout with UPI QR

Challenge: payment complexity and security.

Decision: UPI QR + COD, no card data stored.

Impact: familiar checkout for Indian students.

Order Status Tracker

Challenge: trust drops after payment.

Decision: visual bar: Processing → Shipped → Delivered.

Impact: transparency without extra messages.

Simple Seller Dashboard

Challenge: students need quick, minimal controls.

Decision: add listing, my products, my orders, buyer view.

Impact: easy management for part-time sellers.

Trust Built Into the UI

Challenge: the old system had no trust layer.

Decision: ratings, reviews, chat, tracking surfaced everywhere.

Impact: answers the biggest user concern directly.

08 / Outcome

Outcome

SmarKet turns a scattered, risky, word-of-mouth process into one trusted campus marketplace. Sellers get reach, structure, and safe payments; buyers get discovery, tracking, and confidence; and the campus gets a living showcase of its own student talent.

What's Next & Where It's Heading

Campus commerce sits inside a fast-growing student creator economy, where trust-based, peer-to-peer selling is steadily replacing scattered WhatsApp and Instagram trade. SmarKet is positioned to grow with that shift, from a single campus into a connected network of student marketplaces.

Roadmap

  • Multi-campus rollout
  • Seller analytics & visual reports
  • AI-based product recommendations
  • Integrated delivery & logistics
  • Loyalty, rewards & verified-seller badges
